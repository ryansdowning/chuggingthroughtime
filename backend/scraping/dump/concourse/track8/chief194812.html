Following World War II, transcontinental sleeping car service direct from the East Coast to the West was introduced on several routes. While the Golden State and the Los Angeles Limited were paired with Pennsy and NYC's second-tier trains, Santa Fe's Chief drew the cream of the crop: The 20th Century Limited and the Broadway Limited.

For an example of streamliner-era competition, compare the schedule of this train against that of the Golden State from the same time period, on Track 9.

RULES FOR THE SUPER CHIEF, TRAINS 17 AND 18, AND FOR THE CHIEF, TRAINS 19 AND 20. Extra fare of $15.00 on The Super Chief and $10.00 on The Chief will be charged between Chicago or Kansas City and California points. Only first-class tickets will be honored. Proportionate extra fares will apply between other points at which these stop to take on or discharge passengers.

On Train 19, THE CHIEF, westbound, advance reservations may be made from scheduled and conditional stops Hutchinson and east thereof to Lamy, Albuquerque, Williams, Ash Fork, Barstow and beyond, also from Lamy, Albuquerque and Williams to Barstow and scheduled stops beyond.

On Train 20, THE CHIEF, eastbound, advance reservations may be made from Monrovia, Pomona, Claremont and Upland to Albuquerque, Lamy and Hutchinson and from Albuquerque and Lamy to schedule stops Hutchinson and east.

Other travel may be carried only between intermediate points which are regular stops for these trains as indicated herein, provided Pullman conductor or agent at terminal has unsold Pullman space at train time which can be assigned to passenger through to destination.
