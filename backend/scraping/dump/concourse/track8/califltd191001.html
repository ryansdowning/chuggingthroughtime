In 1910 the California Limited was still Santa Fe's "top of the line". However, starting with the winter of 1911-12 it would be bumped from the top spot by the new, once-a-week Santa Fe de Luxe. The "de Luxe" would operate for only six seasons, until luxury passenger service was curtailed by the United States' entry into World War I. The Limited would again hold the top spot in Santa Fe's operation for almost ten years, until it was permanently relegated to second-tier status by the new Chief of 1926.
