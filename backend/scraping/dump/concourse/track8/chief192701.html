Here is the genesis train for Santa Fe's famous Chief franchise. The brand-new "extra fast, extra fine, extra fare" Chief would depart Dearborn station on November 14, 1926 with the stars of M-G-M's "War Paint" on board. The new daily service included barber, valet, ladies' maid, soda fountain, cigar store and shower bath. Extra fare of $10.00, please.

NOTE—The Chief (No. 20) will stop at stations between Pasadena and San Bernardino, at Ash Fork and Williams to receive interstate passengers destined Pueblo, Colorado Springs and Denver, also Hutchinson and east, and will also stop at Hutchinson, Carrolton, Bucklin, La Plata, Fort Madison, Galesburg, Streator and Joliet to discharge paying passengers from California and Arizona points.
