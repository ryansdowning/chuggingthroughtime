The Gulf, Mobile & Northern's Rebel was not only the first true streamliner in the South, it was one of the first lightweight streamliners anywhere (along with B. & O.'s Royal Blue and Abraham Lincoln, both trainsets of which would eventually be inherited by G. M. & N. successor G. M. & O.) to be built with conventional (non-articulated) construction, allowing extra cars to be switched in and out as needed. According to Johnston and Welsh in The Art of the Streamliner, the American Car & Foundry-built trainsets, styled by Otto Kuhler of B. & O. fame, featured racially segregated coach seating and a sleeper-observation car, with six sections and a drawing-room, staffed by a train hostess.

THE REBEL

The South's first streamlined, air-conditioned train, operates between New Orleans, La., and Jackson, Tenn., via Jackson, Miss. Carries Sleeping Cars and Coaches; also between Mobile and Jackson, Tenn. Hostess in attendance.

A rate of 2 cents a mile, good in both coaches and sleeping cars, is effective between all points on the Gulf Mobile & Northern Railroad.

Sleeping-Observation cars are operated on THE REBEL between Jackson Tenn., and New Orleans, via Jackson, Miss.
