   Between Minneapolis and Los Angeles. (Reclining seats with full length leg rest RESERVED in advance.) (EXTRA FARE between Kansas City and Los Angeles Trains 3 and 4.) (Free pillows.)


NOTE—ROCKET TRAINS—THE ROCKETS are not extra fare trains.  All space in parlor cars and sleeping cars; also in chair cars on Trains 7, 8 and 13 is reserved and individually assigned in advance. Special service charge in Minneapolis-Los Angeles Chair Car on Nos. 17 and 18.

By October of 1964 the Twin Star Rocket had been cut back to a Minneapolis-Fort Worth run; Houston-bound passengers had to transfer to the Burlington's Sam Houston Zephyr for Dallas and points beyond. By July of 1966 the Sam Houston Zephyr was gone. By March of 1967, so was the Twin Star Rocket, although the train numbers were retained under a new name, the Plainsman, on a Minneapolis-Kansas City routing. This service would hang on through June of 1969—but by February of 1970 it too was gone.
