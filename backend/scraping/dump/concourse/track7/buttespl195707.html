Another timetable from the Union Pacific 1957 Summer Tours series; the seasonal Yellowstone Special was a linchpin of the "YT", "YRG" and "YTZ" tours, naturally enough. It was also, at least as of this time period, joined at the hip with the year-round Butte Special, so I have included both trains in this timetable.

The detailed itineraries for the "YT" and "YRG" packages show the tours departing Ogden at 8:55 p.m., five minutes after the departure of No. 35 in this timetable. There are a couple of possible reasons for this; there may have been a minor schedule change in the months after the tour brochure booklet went to press or the U. P. may have operated these tours as a second section following the scheduled train. The arrival times in West Yellowstone and Victor coincide exactly with the published timetable, so it appears that the trains were consolidated at some point along the way.

NOTE for No. 29 at all flag stop stations between Salt Lake City and Pocatello: Conditional stop stations without a timepoint are not stops for No. 29; however, No. 35 will stop at these stations to let off paying passengers or to take for Idaho Falls and beyond.

NOTE: Trains 35, 36, 47 and 48 operate only during Yellowstone Park summer season; June 17 to September 5, inclusive. Trains 29, 30, 477 and 478 operate year-round. Trains 477 and 478 are mixed freight trains with a coach/baggage car and do not operate on Sundays.

NOTE: West Yellowstone, Mont., is gateway to Yellowstone National Park. Regular train service operates only during Yellowstone Park season, June 17 to September 5.

NOTE: This timetable is very ambiguous about the operation of through equipment in the Butte Special. It appears that the sleeper and cafe car operate in Nos. 5 and 6 between Ogden and Salt Lake with the sleeper transferring to/from Nos. 29 and 30 in Salt Lake City. However, this information is not guaranteed; it is suggested that you verify with contemporary sources before committing to your time travel plan.

NOTE: Although the equipment listing for the Yellowstone Special indicates that the Pocatello sleeper terminates in Pocatello, the listing for the Portland Rose states that it continues in that train to and from Portland.
