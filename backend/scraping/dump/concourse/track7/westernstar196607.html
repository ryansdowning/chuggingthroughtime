When the Great Northern upgraded the streamlined Empire Builder with new equipment in 1951 the 1947 equipment was transferred to a new, secondary train: the Western Star. This train served many of the minor stops which the Empire Builder skipped, notably Glacier National Park. By the time of this timetable in the mid-1960s the train's itinerary emphasized the Seattle-St. Paul service, although through equipment did still continue to and from Chicago combined with Burlington trains. By 1971, however, through service to Chicago would end on this train and it would end its days as a Seattle-St. Paul run.

(Spokane, Portland & Seattle Ry. Notes)

EQUIPMENT

All regularly assigned cars are completely Air-Conditioned.

S. P. and S. No. 3-4, G. N. No. 27-28—The Western Star—All classes of tickets honored. Between Spokane and St. Paul—Standard sleeping car. Between Portland and Spokane, de luxe coaches. Between Spokane and Chicago—Day-Night coach with leg rests. Between Spokane and St. Paul, dining lounge car service.

NOTE—No. 3 will stop at any station to discharge revenue passengers from east of Spokane; also at any station west of Pasco to discharge revenue passengers from Spokane; also at any station east of Pasco to receive revenue passengers for Vancouver and Portland. No. 4 will stop at any station to receive revenue passengers destined east of Spokane; also at any station west of Pasco to receive revenue passengers for Spokane; also at any station east of Pasco to discharge revenue passengers from Portland or Vancouver. Stops to receive passengers made only upon advance arrangements through agent. Stops to discharge passengers made upon application to conductor. [There are 2 stations between Vancouver and Portland; 25 stations between Vancouver and Pasco, and 14 stations between Pasco and Spokane which are not shown in this timetable.]
