For about a year following the 1960 merger of the Lackawanna with the Erie, Phoebe continued unchanged. In 1962, for a time, the train was combined with the New York—Chicago Erie-Lackawanna Limited. By August of 1963, the Phoebe Snow had disappeared completely from the Erie-Lackawanna's timetable. But by 1964 Phoebe was back as the no-longer-hyphenated railroad's standard bearer on a New York—Chicago routing via Hornell and Youngstown, Ohio, with through cars to Buffalo from Hornell.
