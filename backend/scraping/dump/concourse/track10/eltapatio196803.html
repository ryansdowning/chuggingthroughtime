After the Broadway Limited and the Panama Limited lost their prestigious all-Pullman status in late 1967, the only all-Pullman trains left in North America were operated south of the border: El Tapatio, shown here, and El Regiomontano, which operated between Monterrey and Mexico City. I have also opted to show in full two of the trains with which El Tapatio interchanged through cars, so you can enjoy three trains for the price of one!

NATIONAL RAILWAYS of MEXICO
THE SYSTEM THAT SERVES ALL MEXICO

EL TAPATIO (All-Pullman.)
No. 5 (Northbound). No. 6 (Southbound).

Sleeping Cars—(Two sections daily).

Mexico City and Nogales (8-Sections, 1 Drawing-room, 2 Compartments) (on No. 1 from Guadalajara) (on No. 2 from Nogales).

Mexico City and Mexicali (8 Sections, 1 Drawing-room, 2 Compartments) (on No. 3 from Guadalajara) (on no. 2 from Mexicali).
Passengers may leave their belongings in the car while laying over in Guadalajara for connections.

Mexico City and Mexicali (8 Sections, 1 Drawing-room, 2 Compartments).

Mexico City and Guadalajara (Trains Nos. 5 and 6, "El Tapatio." Sections, Roomettes, Double Bedrooms, Compartments and Drawing-rooms. (Cars 159-160, 7 Compartments, 2 Drawing-rooms with shower baths).

Mexico City and Guanajuato (10 Roomettes, 6 Double Bedrooms) on Trains 5 and 6 to and from Irapuato and on Trains 281 and 284 between Irapuato and Guanajuato.

Dining Cars—

Mexico City and Guadalajara, also between Guadalajara and Nogales on Pacific R.R. trains Nos. 1-2, and on Trains 3-4 between Guadalajara and Benjamin Hill. No dining car service on Sonora-Baja California trains Nos. 1 and 2 between Benjamin Hill and Mexicali.

Observation Cars—

Mexico City and Guadalajara (Beverages).

Note: Trains Nos. 5 and 6, "El Tapatio," are All-Pullman. No Coaches.
