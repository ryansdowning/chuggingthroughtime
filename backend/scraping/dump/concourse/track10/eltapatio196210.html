NATIONAL RAILWAYS of MEXICO
THE SYSTEM THAT SERVES ALL MEXICO

EL TAPATIO (All-Pullman.)
No. 5 (Northbound). No. 6 (Southbound).

Sleeping Cars—(Two sections daily).

Mexico City and Nogales (8-Sections, 1 Drawing-room, 2 Compartments). Passengers may leave their belongings in the car while laying over in Guadalajara for connections.

Mexico City and Mexicali (8 Sections, 1 Drawing-room, 2 Compartments).

Mexico City and Guadalajara (Trains Nos. 5 and 6, "El Tapatio." Sections, Roomettes, Double Bedrooms, Compartments and Drawing-rooms. (Cars 159-160, 7 Compartments, 2 Drawing-rooms with shower baths).

Mexico City and Zamora (12-Sections, 1 Drawing-room) on Trains 5 and 6 to and from Yurecuaro and on Trains 96 and 95 between Yurecuaro and Zamora.

Mexico City and Guanajuato (12-Sections, 2 Double Bedrooms) on Trains 5 and 6 to and from Irapuato, and on Trains 231 (extra) and 232 between Irapuato and Guanajuato.

Leon-Mexico City, on at Irapuato from train No. 232, southbound. [Note: Probably picked up from León by Train 232 duing the 2 hour layover in Silao. See also comments on El Fronterizo page.]

Dining Cars—

Mexico City and Guadalajara, also between Guadalajara and Nogales on Pacific R.R. trains Nos. 1-2, and on Trains 3-4 between Guadalajara and Benjamin Hill. No dining car service on Sonora-Baja California trains Nos. 1 and 2 between Benjamin Hill and Mexicali.

Observation Cars—

Mexico City and Guadalajara (Beverages).
