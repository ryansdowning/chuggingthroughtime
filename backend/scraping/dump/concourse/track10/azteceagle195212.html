The Aztec Eagle was the principal rail passenger connection between the major U.S. cities and Mexico in the 1950s. At various points in its lifetime (although not on this timetable) it carried through sleepers between St. Louis and Mexico City. I have heard rumors of a direct New York-Mexico City sleeper but I have not yet found such an entry; there were through sleepers from New York to San Antonio in this timetable and a St. Louis-Mexico City all-Pullman train before WWII, but I have as yet seen no through cars from New York to Mexico. Your suggestions are welcome. [Edit—January 2013: Found it. Through Pullmans operated briefly between New York and Mexico City on the P. R.R.-Mo. Pac.-N. de M. Sunshine Special between 1946 and 1948. A tip of the hat to "Harry Foster Malone, Esq." on the Railfan.Net forums; confirmed in Arthur Dubin's More Classic Trains. Timetable for the 1947 Sunshine Special is "in the works."]
